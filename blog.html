Understanding GLP-1 weight loss medications

A quick overview of how GLP-1 therapies work, what to expect, and which questions to ask your healthcare provider.

What are GLP-1 medications?

GLP-1 medications are prescription therapies that mimic a natural hormone involved in appetite regulation and glucose control. They can help people feel full sooner, reduce cravings, and support sustained weight management when paired with nutrition and lifestyle changes.

Who may be a candidate?

Eligibility is based on factors like body mass index, existing health conditions, and medical history. A licensed clinician can determine whether GLP-1 therapy is appropriate for your situation.

  • Individuals with a BMI over 27 with weight-related conditions
  • People seeking clinically supervised weight loss support
  • Patients who have tried lifestyle-only approaches without success

What does the process look like?

Most programs begin with a health intake and brief questionnaire. If you qualify, a provider reviews your information and determines a personalized plan, including ongoing monitoring and adjustments.

Side effects can include nausea, digestive changes, or other symptoms. Always discuss benefits and risks with a healthcare professional before starting any medication.
